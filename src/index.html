What is Personally Identifiable Information?

Personally Identifiable Information is any data that can be used to identify an individual, including names, dates of birth, addresses, contact details, identification numbers, or financial information. This data is commonly used by banks and other institutions to identify customers, but is often inadvertently exposed in a user's social media posts. Financial fraudsters can leverage this data to bypass security checks and gain access to mobile banking services, giving scammers unfettered access to a user's account. To avoid becoming the victim of such fraud, users should take particular care when making posts online. Social Spiders can help to indicate where Personally Identifiable Information has previously been exposed on a user's social media.


Dummy Scan

A dummy scan is a scan done on the Social Spiders Twitter account, simulating a private social media account. It shows the different types of personal data can be exposed in your social media and how it can be found in some obscure places.

Be sure to have a look at all the posts to see which ones do have personal information in it and which ones don't.


Start a scan

To start a scan on your account:

  1. Hit the button for the platform your account is on above.
  2. Login to the account you want to scan if prompted.
  3. Grant permissions for us to be able to read your posts.
  4. See how secure you really are.


What to do with the result

Have a think about which posts are flagged, consider who can see it and what they can do with that information. If any of the posts flags exposes personal information to people you do not want accessing it we highly suggest you remove the post.

We also suggest you have a look at the other posts which are not flagged, there are some things we are unable to scan for, so make sure to double check any pictures.


Privacy policy

We do not store any data, once you leave the results page all the posts we have gathered are gone to the eather.
